# Empirical Claims and Evidence

This document outlines key empirical claims derived from the provided FRED data, along with their supporting evidence.

## Claim 1: Significant Nominal Growth in New Jersey Housing Prices

**Claim:** The New Jersey State House Price Index (NJSTHPI) experienced a near doubling in nominal value between early 2015 and late 2025.
**Evidence:** The `housing_price_index` (NJSTHPI) increased from a starting value of 472.09 on 2015-01-01 to an ending value of 939.31 on 2025-10-01, representing a total change of 99.0%.

## Claim 2: Substantial Inflation Over the Study Period

**Claim:** The Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPIAUCSL) showed a notable increase, indicating significant inflation across the study period.
**Evidence:** The `cpi` (CPIAUCSL) rose from 234.75 on 2015-01-01 to 332.41 on 2026-04-01, reflecting a 41.6% increase.

## Claim 3: Decline in New Jersey's Unemployment Rate

**Claim:** New Jersey's unemployment rate (NJURN) saw a considerable decrease over the decade, suggesting improving labor market conditions.
**Evidence:** The `unemployment_rate` (NJURN) declined from 6.90% on 2015-01-01 to 4.70% on 2026-03-01, marking a -31.9% change.

## Claim 4: Real Housing Price Growth Outpaced General Inflation

**Claim:** New Jersey's nominal housing price growth significantly outstripped the general rate of inflation, indicating a substantial real increase in housing values and a potential decrease in affordability.
**Evidence:** While the `cpi` increased by 41.6% from 2015-01-01 to 2026-04-01, the `housing_price_index` concurrently surged by 99.0% from 2015-01-01 to 2025-10-01. This nearly 2.4x difference in growth rates (99.0% vs 41.6%) implies that housing became considerably more expensive in real terms.
