# Empirical Claims and Evidence: NJ Labor Market

This document outlines key empirical claims regarding the New Jersey labor market, grounded in the provided FRED unemployment data (NJURN and UNRATE) from 2015-2026.

## Claim 1: Significant Decline in New Jersey's Unemployment Rate

New Jersey's unemployment rate experienced a substantial decline over the analyzed period, falling from 6.90% on January 1, 2015, to 4.70% on March 1, 2026. This represents a 31.9% reduction in the unemployment rate.

*   **Evidence:** FRED API series NJURN data.
    *   Start value: 6.90% (2015-01-01)
    *   End value: 4.70% (2026-03-01)
    *   Calculated percentage change: -31.9%

## Claim 2: National Unemployment Rate Also Declined, but Less Sharply in Percentage Terms

The national unemployment rate also showed a positive trend, decreasing from 5.70% on January 1, 2015, to 4.30% on April 1, 2026. This constitutes a 24.6% reduction in the national rate.

*   **Evidence:** FRED API series UNRATE data.
    *   Start value: 5.70% (2015-01-01)
    *   End value: 4.30% (2026-04-01)
    *   Calculated percentage change: -24.6%

## Claim 3: New Jersey Outpaced the Nation in Relative Unemployment Reduction

Comparatively, New Jersey achieved a more significant percentage reduction in its unemployment rate (31.9%) than the national average (24.6%) over their respective observed periods.

*   **Evidence:**
    *   Percentage change in NJURN: -31.9%
    *   Percentage change in UNRATE: -24.6%

## Claim 4: Persistent Higher Unemployment in New Jersey Relative to the Nation

Despite New Jersey's strong percentage reduction, its unemployment rate remained higher than the national average throughout the period. New Jersey began with a higher rate (6.90% vs. 5.70% in early 2015) and concluded the observation period still above the national rate (4.70% in March 2026 vs. 4.30% in April 2026).

*   **Evidence:**
    *   NJURN (2015-01-01): 6.90%
    *   UNRATE (2015-01-01): 5.70%
    *   NJURN (2026-03-01): 4.70%
    *   UNRATE (2026-04-01): 4.30%
