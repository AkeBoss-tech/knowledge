# Verification Certificate: Northeast Housing Comparison Analysis

## What was analyzed (research questions answered)

This analysis examines the housing price index (HPI) trajectories for New Jersey (NJSTHPI), New York (NYSTHPI), and Connecticut (CTSTHPI) from January 2015 to October 2025, utilizing data from the FRED API. The primary objective was to compare the performance of these states, identify the strongest post-COVID surge, normalize indices for fair comparison, and observe patterns of convergence or divergence.

## Sources backing the claims

The following FRED series were used as sources for the analysis:

*   **New Jersey State House Price Index (NJSTHPI):** https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/NJSTHPI
*   **New York State House Price Index (NYSTHPI):** https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/NYSTHPI
*   **Connecticut State House Price Index (CTSTHPI):** https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/CTSTHPI

## Key quantitative findings

*   By October 2025, New Jersey's normalized HPI reached **198.96**, indicating a **99.0%** increase from its 2015 baseline.
*   New York's normalized HPI reached **198.44** by October 2025, representing a **98.4%** increase from its 2015 baseline.
*   Connecticut's normalized HPI reached **183.50** by October 2025, showing an **83.5%** increase from its 2015 baseline.
*   New York consistently maintained the highest absolute HPI values throughout the period, starting at **579.72** in 2015-01-01 and ending at **1150.44** in 2025-10-01.
