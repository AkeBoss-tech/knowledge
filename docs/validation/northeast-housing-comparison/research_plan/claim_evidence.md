# Empirical Claims and Evidence: Northeast State Housing Price Comparison (2015-2025)

This document outlines key empirical claims derived from the provided housing price index data for New Jersey (NJSTHPI), New York (NYSTHPI), and Connecticut (CTSTHPI) spanning from January 2015 to October 2025.

---

## Claim 1: New Jersey experienced the strongest overall percentage growth in housing prices.

**Evidence:**
*   **New Jersey (NJSTHPI):** Increased from an initial index value of 472.09 in 2015-01-01 to 939.31 in 2025-10-01, representing a **99.0% change**.
*   **New York (NYSTHPI):** Increased from an initial index value of 579.72 in 2015-01-01 to 1150.44 in 2025-10-01, representing a **98.4% change**.
*   **Connecticut (CTSTHPI):** Increased from an initial index value of 386.30 in 2015-01-01 to 708.82 in 2025-10-01, representing an **83.5% change**.

New Jersey's 99.0% growth slightly surpasses New York's 98.4%, making it the state with the highest relative increase over the entire period.

---

## Claim 2: When normalized to a 2015 baseline, New Jersey and New York show highly similar housing price trajectories, significantly outpacing Connecticut.

**Evidence (Normalized Index, 2015-01-01 = 100):**
*   **New Jersey (NJSTHPI):** Normalized end value by 2025-10-01 is (939.31 / 472.09) * 100 = **198.96**.
*   **New York (NYSTHPI):** Normalized end value by 2025-10-01 is (1150.44 / 579.72) * 100 = **198.44**.
*   **Connecticut (CTSTHPI):** Normalized end value by 2025-10-01 is (708.82 / 386.30) * 100 = **183.50**.

Both New Jersey and New York experienced approximately a 98-99% increase from their 2015 baseline values, reaching nearly double their initial indices. Connecticut, however, saw a more modest increase of 83.5% from its baseline.

---

## Claim 3: New York maintained the highest absolute housing price index values throughout the period, followed by New Jersey and then Connecticut.

**Evidence (Absolute Index Values):**
*   **2015-01-01:**
    *   NYSTHPI: 579.72
    *   NJSTHPI: 472.09
    *   CTSTHPI: 386.30
*   **2025-10-01:**
    *   NYSTHPI: 1150.44
    *   NJSTHPI: 939.31
    *   CTSTHPI: 708.82

New York consistently holds the highest index values, indicating higher absolute housing prices compared to its neighbors. The ranking of states by absolute index value (NY > NJ > CT) remained consistent from 2015 to 2025.
